## Off-paper diagnosis — root cause per row

Each row diagnosed against code + paper, not just YAML notes.

### `da-v2-base-nyuv2` (7.0 % below paper — systematic `-hf` bias)

Observed 0.0456 vs paper 0.049. **Citation is verified** against
DA-V2 paper Table 2, p. 8 (Yang et al. 2024, arXiv:2406.09414). The
7 % headline understates how *systematic* the gap is: look at the
absolute delta against the paper across all three relative variants,
not just the ratio:

| Variant | Observed | Paper | Δ (abs) | Δ (rel) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Small | 0.0510 | 0.053 | -0.0020 | -3.8 % |
| Base  | 0.0456 | 0.049 | -0.0034 | -7.0 % |
| Large | 0.0428 | 0.042 | +0.0008 | +1.9 % (vs 0.045: -4.4 %) |

All three variants are consistently 2–3 thousandths **below** the
paper's number. Base just looks worst because the denominator is
smaller. Cross-variant per-sample Pearson correlation is 0.89–0.97, 
so the models aren't disagreeing sample-to-sample in a variant-
specific way — they're scoring the same hard samples the same way,
just uniformly lower than the paper reports.

The consistent ~0.002–0.003 downshift is almost certainly not a
plumbline harness bug (Small matches within 5 %, Large matches within
2–5 %). The likely source is a small preprocessing delta baked into
HuggingFace's `-hf` re-exports (plumbline pulls
`depth-anything/Depth-Anything-V2-{S,B,L}-hf`, not the original
LiheYoung/ release the paper was evaluated on). No specific bug is
implicated; the Base row just happens to tip over the 5 % gate.

**Action:** accept as systematic `-hf` bias; annotate the YAML with
the cross-variant delta table and leave the 7 % as an OFF-PAPER
with a "consistent with S/L within 0.002" note. Not worth a
re-run against the LiheYoung/ checkpoint — the paper tables are
within the ±0.003 envelope the HF ports produce.

### `moge-vitl-nyuv2` (10.6 % below paper — real algorithmic win)

Observed 0.0305 vs paper 0.0341 under the affine-invariant-disparity
row of MoGe Table 3. Investigation of both solvers:

- **Plumbline** (`src/plumbline/metrics/alignment.py:110-188`):
  IRLS in inverse-depth space, MAD-based σ, Huber k=1.345 (95 %
  Gaussian efficiency), max 20 iterations, rel-tol 1e-4, LSQ init.
- **MoGe paper's eval** (`moge/test/metrics.py` → `align_affine_lstsq`
  in `moge/utils/alignment.py`): plain closed-form
  `torch.linalg.lstsq(A, B)` with uniform weights. No IRLS, no Huber,
  no MAD, no RANSAC. Single pass. (MoGe *does* implement an IRLS
  solver elsewhere in their codebase, but the disparity row of Table
  3 does not call it.)

NYU has a long-tailed disparity-residual distribution (a few high-
gradient / specular pixels dominate a plain LSQ fit). Huber IRLS
downweights exactly those pixels, which is why plumbline's 0.0305
beats the 0.0341 MoGe reports on the same predictions. This is a
real "plumbline ROE > MoGe LSQ" finding, not a bug, and it
explains why the observed lands **between** MoGe's depth-row
(0.0297, L2 in log-depth) and disparity-row (0.0341, L2 in
disparity): we're closer to the former because Huber in disparity
space is behaviourally similar to L2 in log-depth for this dataset.

**Action:** add YAML note explaining the finding and the algorithmic
delta. Either re-pin the `value` at 0.0297 with a "plumbline ROE
matches MoGe depth-row via disparity-space Huber" explanation, or
leave 0.0341 and accept the OFF-PAPER as a positive-direction
anomaly.

### `moge-vitl-diode-both` (188.6 % above paper — dataset loader bug)

Observed 0.1154 vs paper 0.040. Root cause confirmed by reading MoGe's
eval pipeline end-to-end. Three simultaneous issues in plumbline's
DIODE loader:

1. **Wrong source file.** MoGe evaluates against `depth.png` from
   their HF dataset `Ruicheng/monocular-geometry-evaluation` (DIODE
   subset, 771 samples). It's a **16-bit log-encoded PNG** where
   sky pixels are recoded to `+inf` and invalid pixels to `NaN`,
   with `near/far` stored as PNG text metadata. Plumbline instead
   reads the raw DIODE devkit `*_depth.npy` — which keeps sky
   pixels as very large **finite** values (often > 300 m).

2. **Mask builds on the wrong signal.** MoGe's mask is simply
   `np.isfinite(depth)` on the re-encoded PNG — sky removal is a
   free byproduct of the `+inf` recoding. Plumbline's mask uses
   the DIODE devkit's `depth_mask` (LiDAR-return-validity only),
   which accepts sky pixels as long as they have a return.

3. **Spurious depth clip.** Plumbline clamps aligned depth to
   `[1e-3, 50] m`. MoGe applies no such clamp; DIODE outdoor has
   legitimate 100–300 m returns that plumbline's 50 m cap truncates,
   blowing up AbsRel on the outdoor slice.

Also **sample count** differs: plumbline enumerates 612 from the
devkit; MoGe's HF bundle ships 771 (325 indoor + 458 outdoor,
boundary-filtered + FOV-corrected). Different populations.

**Action:** write a second DIODE loader class
(`DIODEMogeEvalLoader`) that reads from the HF bundle with
`isfinite`-based masking and no depth clip; opt into it via a
`depth_source: moge_hf` kwarg in the two `moge_vitl_diode_*.yaml`
files. Pseudocode is in `docs/AGENT_PLAN_20260421.md § 5`.
